CLASSES

Puppy Preschool & Socialization

  • 6 weeks, weekly
  • 1 hour sessions

Basic obedience commands will be taught, manners are introduced in this class with a strong emphasis on preventing unwanted behavior problems. Class provides positive interactions with both people and other puppies. Puppies will enjoy accomplishing an obstacle course. Held indoors. For all breeds of puppies 8 to 24 weeks of age.

This is an extremely important class for puppies and their owners. Puppy preschool is invaluable in avoiding future behavior problems and making your training experience much smoother. The class will be a very positive introduction to training. Puppies will experience positive interactions with both people and dogs in a safe controlled environment and experience going thru an obstacle course. Class will provide positive socialization which is one of the most fundamental aspects of raising a confidant, well-balanced dog. All behaviors will be discussed. Puppies are required to have at least 2 DHLPP and Bordetella is strongly suggested. Proof of vaccinations is required and will be checked continuously throughout. Yes we accept Titers. First meeting is held without puppies. Puppy classes are held indoors. Six weekly one hour sessions.

Beginner's/Novice Dog Obedience

  • 6 weeks, weekly
  • 1 hour sessions

All obedience exercises will be taught. This basic training is important in keeping your dog safe and is needed to make a dog a good companion. Behavior problems will be addressed. For all dogs 4 months and older.

A class designed for all dogs 4 months or older. The basic class emphasizes the essential training commands needed to make a dog a good companion. All basic obedience exercises will be taught, such as "heeling" (walking on the leash without pulling or tugging) with automatic sits when handler comes to a stop, teaching your dog to "come" on command, "sit" and "down" stays up to a leash length away with distractions, the "stand/sit" exercise, good for grooming, bathing, greeting people and/or veterinarian checks. Behavior problems will be addressed such as, jumping, digging, barking, chewing and more! First meeting held without dogs. Bring a chair to first meeting. Six weekly one hour sessions.

Beginner's/Novice Dog Obedience

  • 6 weeks, weekly
  • 1 hour sessions

Advance your dog to the next level. We work on perfecting and strengthening obedience commands to refine these skills and work towards performing these exercises consistently at home or out in public where we need it the most. Anyone who is thinking about going on to earn the Canine Good Citizen Award should complete this class.

Advance your dog’s training to the next level. This class helps both dog and owners to reinforce the lessons learned in Beginner’s Obedience to a more advanced level. We work on perfecting and strengthening commands with distractions and distance work (15 foot lead). Intermediate is to refine these skills and work towards performing these exercises consistently at home or out in public where we need it the most. At this level the owner increases the dogs’ predictability and consistency in obedience. Taking your dog through this level is a good goal for all pet dog owners and really sets a lifetime foundation for good behavior and good communication with your dog. Anyone who is thinking about going on to earn the Canine Good Citizen (CGC) Award should complete this class. Intermediate obedience is taught to any dog that has been successful with the basic obedience commands. Pre-requisite dogs must have successfully completed a Beginner’s Dog Obedience class, (attending at least 5 of 6 lessons). Bring dogs to all meetings. Six week 1 hours sessions.

Canine Good Citizenship (CGC)

Canine Good Citizenship (CGC) includes 10 tests that prove that the dog is able to be in public safely. The CGC is a per-requisite to virtually every Therapy Dog Organization in the United States. This test is then registered with the American Kennel Club (AKC) and a certificate is awarded. Dogs must be one year or older.

The Canine Good Citizen (CGC) program teaches good manners to dogs and responsible ownership to their owners. The CGC is a per-requisite to virtually every Therapy Dog Organization in the United States. The purpose of the CGC Program is to ensure that our favorite companion, the dog, can be a respected member of the community because he has been trained to be well behaved in the home, public places, and in the presence of other dogs. Class is open to all breeds and mixed breeds. COMING SOON

Canine Good Citizen Test Sponsored by the American Kennel Club (AKC)

This program consists of a ten part test that must be passed in order to achieve this award, which also can be applied for as a title. This 4 week class is geared toward training for and passing the test to achieve this award or title. This class is subject change locations weekly in order to practice obedience skills in public with distractions and provides socialization.

At the end of this class you will be eligible for the test which will consist of the following items:

  1. Accepting a friendly stranger
  2. Sitting politely for petting
  3. Appearance and Grooming
  4. Out for a walk (loose leash walking)
  5. Walking through a crowd
  6. Sit and Down on Command and Staying in Place
  7. Coming when called
  8. Reaction to Another Dog
  9. Reaction to distractions
  10. Supervised 3 minute out of sight

Requirements for this class: 6 foot leash, flat collar, martingale or training collar. No prong collars, no electric collars, no easy walk harnesses, or any other harness.

All dogs must provide proof of current Rabies vaccination.
